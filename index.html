About

A hard-touring troubadour surviving on raw talent and a heartfelt folk-rock repertoire, Thomas Hinds performs full-time across the country, averaging 70,000 miles annually.

A singer/songwriter from northern Georgia, Thomas began writing in his early twenties but stepped away to raise a family. When his children went out on their own, he committed to performing full-time starting in 2019—and never looked back.

Relocating from New Mexico to North Georgia, his travels took him to Alabama's legendary FAME Studios, where he recorded Where Do We Go From Here with six musicians who helped flesh out his sound—bringing together the lilting melodies of Americana with the swampy Muscle Shoals groove.

His latest album reached #86 on the top 100 Americana Album charts, with three songs charting in Georgia's top ten. He performed at SXSW 2024 in Austin, Texas. Critics note his sound "sounds like a cross between the ethereal crooning of Jeff Buckley and the down-to-earth angst of early Wilco."

Thomas is also the author of Ghost of a River (2022). Follow his poetry and prose on Hindsight Musings.
